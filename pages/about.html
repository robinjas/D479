About Taniti

Taniti is a small, tropical island in the Pacific. While the island has an area of less than 500 square miles, the terrain is varied and includes both sandy and rocky beaches, a small but safe harbor, lush tropical rainforests, and a mountainous interior that includes a small, active volcano.

Taniti has an indigenous population of about 20,000. Until a recent increase in tourism, most of the Tanitian economy was dominated by fishing or agriculture.

Local Culture

The people of Taniti are known for their warm hospitality and rich cultural traditions. Many younger Tanitians speak fluent English, while in rural areas, especially among older residents, the local language is more prevalent.

Economy

While traditionally reliant on fishing and agriculture, Taniti's economy is increasingly diversifying with the growth of tourism. This shift is bringing new opportunities and challenges to the island.

Natural Beauty

From pristine beaches to lush rainforests and an active volcano, Taniti offers a diverse range of natural wonders for visitors to explore and enjoy.
